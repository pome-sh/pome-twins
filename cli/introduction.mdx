## Introduction

Pome is the safety layer for your AI agents.

You can use Pome to understand, test, and validate how AI agents behave before
they ever interact with real users.

Pome builds simulations of your agent's interactions against deterministic scenarios. Pome
replaces guesswork with certainty by providing the deep visibility required to take
autonomous agents from experimental toys to production-ready tools.

## Simulation Evaluations

Pome builds simulations to understand how an AI agent will interact with the real
world using **stateful digital twins** in sandboxes. In these simulations, Pome
automatically runs scenarios to see how agents behave, where they fail, and how they
can be improved.

A **scenario** is a specific situation we put the agent in:

- A **starting world state** — the twin's seeded baseline (repos, charges, channels, etc.)
- A **task to complete** — the prompt the agent receives
- The **real services it must call** — GitHub, Stripe, Slack, and more via twins

By varying these inputs, Pome surfaces edge cases that normal tests never reach.

Pome brings deterministic safety checks and deep visibility into how your agent
works — and aims to be the fastest way to build safer agents. Runs, traces, and
scores live on the dashboard at [pome.sh](https://pome.sh).
